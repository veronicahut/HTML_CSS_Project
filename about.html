about me

My programming journey began when I returned to school to study computer science while working part-time as an environmental lab technician. I later transitioned into a full-time role as a data technician in the IT department of the same company. After earning a certificate as a programmer-analyst, I was promoted to IT Specialist and eventually to IT Project Manager. I then chose to pursue an A.B. degree in Computer Programming while continuing to work full time. When I learned that my position would be eliminated, I decided to follow my passion and pursue a career as a computer programmer.

I enjoy developing applications and designing intuitive, user-friendly interfaces. I'm passionate about solving problems creatively and fostering positive experiences for users. I strive to create interfaces that leave users feeling confident and satisfied with their experience. I also have a strong appreciation for learning new platforms, tools, and programming languages.

My work experience includes developing .NET applications that transform data into specific deliverable formats, as well as student projects involving both software development and interface design using various languages and IDEs. I have thrived both independently and as part of a team. While working remotely, I was able to maintain focus and productivity, and I've also collaborated successfully in academic and professional environments.

I love problem-solving and the challenges and rewards it brings. I draw inspiration from nature and outdoor activities. In my free time, I enjoy running, hiking, reading, baking, spending time with my family, and traveling.
