about me

My programming and design journey began when I returned to school to study programming while working as a part time environmental lab technician. Staying with the same company, I took a full time position as a data technician in the IT department.I earned a certificate as a programmer-analyst and my employer promoted me to IT Specialist and later to IT project manager. Deciding to return to school for an A.B. degree in computer programming, I worked full time while taking classes. Upon realizing that my position was going to be eliminated, I opted to follow my goals and search for a position as a programmer.

Work I like to do includes developing applications and designing user-friendly interfaces. I enjoy using my creativity to solve problems and creating a positive environment. I am interested in providing a user experience that leaves the users feeling confident about their tasks and feeling good about their experience. I have an appreciation for learning new platforms and tools, as well as new languages.

Work I have done includes developing .NET applications to manipulate data into a deliverable format and student projects in development and design with various programming languages and platforms. I have been successful working individually and with a team. When I worked from home, I was able to focus and stay on task. And I have been able to effectively collaborate in various situations at school and work.

I get many inspirations from nature and being outdoors. Some of my hobbies include running, hiking, reading, and baking. I also enjoy spending time with my family and traveling in my free time.
