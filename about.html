about me

My programming journey began when I returned to school to study this topic while working as a part time environmental lab technician. Remaining with the same company, I took a full time position as a data technician in the IT department. I earned a certificate as a programmer-analyst and I was promoted to IT Specialist and then later to IT project manager. Deciding to return to school for an A.B. degree in computer programming, I worked full time while taking classes. Upon realizing that my position was going to be eliminated, I opted to follow my goals and search for a position as a computer programmer.

Work I like to do includes developing applications and designing user-friendly interfaces. I enjoy using my creativity to solve problems and creating a positive environment. I am interested in providing a user experience that leaves the users feeling confident about their tasks and feeling good about their experience. I have an appreciation for learning new platforms and tools, as well as new languages.

Work I have done includes developing .NET applications to manipulate data into specific deliverable formats and student projects in development and design with various programming languages and IDEs. I have been successful working individually and with a team. When I worked from home, I was able to focus and stay on task. I alko have been able to effectively collaborate in various situations at school and work.

I love problem solving and the challenges and rewards it brings. I get many inspirations from nature and being outdoors. Some of my hobbies include running, hiking, reading, and baking. I also enjoy spending time with my family and traveling in my free time.
